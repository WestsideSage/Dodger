# Brief 4.6 — War Room (Playoff Flair)

**Phase 8 Claude Design handoff. Logic settled in Phases 1–7.**

---

## 1. Teardown

**Components:**
- `frontend/src/components/LeagueContext.tsx` — the Standings page (`/api/standings`), which renders the full standings table, the "Need" callout, the race summary strip, and the League Wire.
- `frontend/src/components/standings/PlayoffBracket.tsx` — the playoff bracket panel (`/api/playoffs/bracket`), embedded in the Standings page via `useApiResource<PlayoffBracketResponse>('/api/playoffs/bracket')`.

**Backing types:**
- `StandingsResponse`: `{ season_id, standings: StandingRow[], recent_matches?, total_weeks, current_week, user_games_remaining?, playoff_spots, is_offseason? }`
- `StandingRow`: `{ club_id, club_name, wins, losses, draws, points, elimination_differential, is_user_club, latest_approach?, program_archetype?, program_trajectory_label? }`
- `PlayoffBracketResponse`: `{ active, season_id?, format?, status?, seeds?, rounds?, champion_club_id?, champion_club_name?, player_club_id? }`
- `PlayoffBracketMatch`: `{ match_id, home_club_id, home_club_name, away_club_id, away_club_name, home_survivors, away_survivors, winner_club_id, status, decided_by?, narrative_note? }`
- `PlayoffSeed`: `{ seed, club_id, club_name, wins, losses, draws, is_player_club }`

**Note on naming:** The Standings surface already uses the "War Room" name inside `Headline.tsx` (the aftermath debrief kicker). The §4.6 target is the **Standings page** — the live-season/playoff information hub accessed from the navigation — not the aftermath header component. During the playoff phase, this page is where the player tracks seedings, bracket progress, and their elimination scenario. The synthesis confirms: "wants more flair, especially for playoffs (it is the playoffs)."

**What exists today:**

The Standings page has these sections (regular season):
1. A race summary strip (left/right stat badges built from `buildRaceSummary`).
2. A "Need" callout box (`buildNeedCopy`) — action/outcome/helper copy that changes by the player's rank vs the playoff line.
3. The full standings table: rank, club, W/L/D, points, survivor diff bar, program approach badge, trajectory label. Rows expand in place to a `ClubHistoryLane` when clicked.
4. A "League Wire" wire-service row showing recent match results.
5. The `PlayoffBracket` panel: appears when `bracket.active === true`. Shows a seedings strip + a two-column grid (Semifinals, Championship Final, Champion card).

In the **playoff phase** (`bracket.active === true`, `data.is_offseason === false`):
- The bracket panel renders below the standings table.
- The standings table still shows all clubs' full-season records.
- The race summary strip shows "SEASON CONCLUDED / PREPARING FOR NEXT SEASON" during offseason, or the live race data during regular season.
- No visual transition or heightened framing distinguishes the playoff context from the regular-season standings experience.

**What is silent / redundant today:**

- When the bracket is active, the full standings table (12 rows) still renders with full weight — the player cares about 4 seeds, not all 12 clubs during playoffs. The standings table becomes largely irrelevant context during postseason but is still the primary element on the page.
- The `PlayoffBracket` panel is positioned below the standings table and the wire. A player entering the playoffs has to scroll past regular-season data to reach the playoff bracket.
- The bracket seedings strip (`playoff-seed-chip`) lists seeds with W/L/D records — correct, but no visual distinction between "my club" and rivals beyond an `is-user` CSS class (which applies some styling).
- `PlayoffBracketMatch` carries `decided_by` ("overtime" / "seed_tiebreaker") and `narrative_note` — the `decided_by` renders as a small cyan "OT" or "SEED" chip, but `narrative_note` is only exposed as a `title` attribute (tooltip-only, invisible on mobile).
- The "Champion" card in the bracket renders a trophy emoji and the club name. This is the most emotionally significant element on the page (the player either just won or just learned who beat them) but it is in the third column of a three-column grid, visually equivalent to the semifinal column.
- No "you are eliminated" or "you advanced" framing when the player's club is in a played match in the bracket.
- Program archetype chips (`program_archetype`) and trajectory labels (`program_trajectory_label`) in the standings table are rendered as badge text — they are V12 AI identity features that add richness, but during playoffs the opponent-scouting use case is their value; the design should preserve them.
- The need/race copy (`buildNeedCopy`, `buildRaceSummary`) is authored for regular-season scenarios (`win next, hold top 4`, etc.). These are stale and potentially misleading once the regular season ends and the playoff bracket drives outcomes.

**Phase 1–7 changes relevant here:** None of Phases 1–7 directly changed the Standings or PlayoffBracket components. The playoff bracket mechanic (including `decided_by` and `narrative_note`) and standings table were shipped in earlier milestones (V2-F, V13). This brief's redesign is purely visual/hierarchy.

## 2. Information Hierarchy

**Primary (playoff phase):** The bracket — seedings, matchups, scores of played matches, and the player's own position in the bracket. When `bracket.active === true`, the bracket should lead, not trail.

**Primary (regular season):** The race — the player's standing relative to the playoff cut, rendered through the existing need/race copy. The standings table supports this, not replaces it.

**Secondary (playoff phase):** The full standings table — kept as context for the completed regular season, but demoted visually so the bracket leads.

**Secondary (playoff phase):** `narrative_note` for decided-by-overtime or seed-tiebreaker matches — this copy explains a contested result and belongs in a visible element, not a `title` attribute.

**Cut or demote (playoff phase):**
- The regular-season race summary strip (`buildRaceSummary`) and need callout (`buildNeedCopy`) during playoffs — these are stale (the race is over). Either hide them or replace with a "regular season concluded" summary.
- The League Wire (recent matches) during playoffs — it may not surface playoff matches cleanly. Consider replacing it with a playoff-specific match results ticker or hiding it.
- `narrative_note` as a tooltip-only `title` attribute — it must render as visible text.

## 3. Data Inventory

**From `StandingsResponse`:**
- `standings`: `StandingRow[]` — each carries `club_id, club_name, wins, losses, draws, points, elimination_differential, is_user_club, latest_approach?, program_archetype?, program_trajectory_label?`
- `playoff_spots`: integer — the playoff cutline
- `total_weeks`, `current_week`: integers — season progress
- `user_games_remaining`: optional integer — games the player still has to play (byes excluded)
- `is_offseason`: optional boolean — true after the playoff phase ends
- `recent_matches`: optional `RecentMatchSummary[]` — `{ match_id, week, summary, winner_name }`

**From `PlayoffBracketResponse`:**
- `active`: boolean — true when playoffs are in progress or concluded
- `seeds`: `PlayoffSeed[]` — `{ seed, club_id, club_name, wins, losses, draws, is_player_club }`
- `rounds`: `PlayoffBracketRound[]` — each has `round: 'semifinal'|'final'` and `matches: PlayoffBracketMatch[]`
- `PlayoffBracketMatch`: `{ match_id, home_club_id, home_club_name, away_club_id, away_club_name, home_survivors, away_survivors, winner_club_id, status: 'played'|'scheduled', decided_by?: 'overtime'|'seed_tiebreaker'|'regulation'|null, narrative_note?: string|null }`
- `champion_club_id`: optional string
- `champion_club_name`: optional string
- `player_club_id`: optional string

**Computed at render time:**
- Whether the player's club is in the bracket: check `seeds.find(s => s.is_player_club)`
- Whether the player's club has a played match with a result: check `rounds[*].matches[*]` for player's `home_club_id` or `away_club_id` with `status === 'played'`
- Player outcome (advanced vs eliminated) in a played match: compare `winner_club_id` to `player_club_id`
- Whether `narrative_note` should be shown: `decided_by` is not `'regulation'` and `narrative_note` is non-null

---

## 4. Constraints

- Mobile viewport 390×844, no horizontal overflow. The current bracket uses a three-column CSS grid (`playoff-bracket-grid`) — at 390px this must scroll horizontally or collapse to a stacked layout. The brief should note this is the primary mobile challenge for the bracket.
- Semantic markup: the bracket rounds should be marked with `<section>` and headings. The seedings strip should be a `<ol>` (ordered by seed). Currently chips are rendered as `<span>` elements — these should have appropriate roles.
- No new API endpoint. The bracket uses the existing `/api/playoffs/bracket` endpoint.
- No routing change. The War Room is the Standings screen at its existing URL.
- "Explain, don't decide" — the bracket results are engine facts; `narrative_note` is the engine's explanation of a contested result. Render it verbatim.
- The `.is-user` class on seed chips and `is_player_club` in bracket match rows must continue to be machine-readable (kept for e2e test assertions).

---

## 5. Success Criteria

1. When `bracket.active === true`, the player sees the bracket without scrolling. The bracket leads; the standings table is below or toggled.
2. `narrative_note` for overtime/seed-tiebreaker results renders as visible body text, not a `title` attribute tooltip.
3. The player's own bracket outcome (advanced / eliminated) is communicated with a distinct visual treatment on their match card, not just a highlighted row.
4. The regular-season race summary and need copy are hidden or replaced when the bracket is active and the regular season is concluded.
5. The bracket renders without horizontal overflow at 390px. If it cannot fit three columns, it must reflow gracefully (e.g., stacked rounds or a swipeable column layout — designer's choice, but no clipping).
6. The champion card in the bracket is visually distinct from the semifinal columns — it is the climax of the screen, not a third column of equal weight.
7. No horizontal overflow in the standings table at 390px (existing `minWidth` columns may need mobile adaptation).
