# Brief 4.3 — Bye Week Aftermath

**Phase 8 Claude Design handoff. Logic settled in Phases 1–7.**

---

## 1. Teardown

**Component:** `frontend/src/components/MatchWeek.tsx` — the `renderPostSimMode` function, reached when the week was a bye (`plan.is_bye === true`). No dedicated component file; the bye path is a conditional variant of the regular match aftermath.
**Relevant child components:** `Headline`, `FalloutGrid`, `ReplayTimeline` (bye variant), `AftermathActionBar`.
**Backing type:** `Aftermath` in `frontend/src/types.ts`.

**What the bye path looks like today:**

The `aftermath.match_card` is `null` on a bye week, so the `MatchScoreHero` and `PrimaryFactorCard` do not render. The `Headline` renders the server-authored `aftermath.headline` string (e.g., "Bye week. Your squad rests."). The `FalloutGrid` renders with `byeRecovery` (not `match_card` data), and the `ReplayTimeline` renders with `isBye={true}` which changes its collapse-bar label to "BYE WEEK REPORT." The `AftermathActionBar` appears at stage 4.

**What is silent / redundant today:**

- The `Headline` component always renders "War Room · Wk N Debrief" regardless of whether a match happened. On a bye, this is incoherent — there was no match and no "war room" debrief to speak of. The kicker and sub-label need a bye-specific framing.
- `FalloutGrid` renders three cards: "Training Impact" (uses `developmentFeedback` or the `byeRecovery.summary`), "Standings Shift", and "Prospect Pulse". On a bye, the "Training Impact" card is the only truly relevant one — standings shift still makes sense (other teams' results changed the table), but "Prospect Pulse" (recruit interest movement) is often unchanged on a bye and reads as noise.
- The `byeRecovery` object's `summary` string surfaces inside the "Training Impact" card as a `<small>` tag — it is buried under `developmentFeedback` when that exists, and appears only after `developmentFeedback.progress`.
- The `ReplayTimeline` shows "BYE WEEK REPORT · N moments" but for a bye there are no `moment_events` (moments are match-engine outputs). The timeline renders an empty collapsed state when `beats.length === 0 && moments.length === 0`, which means it disappears entirely. That is correct but the parent should not reserve space for it on a bye.
- No explicit rest/recovery framing communicates why a bye matters strategically — the "Training Impact" label does not tell the player which players benefited from the rest.

**Phase 1–7 changes relevant here:**
- Phase 3 (D3): bye week auto-clears both the `scout` and `confirm_lineup` readiness gates. This means the bye aftermath occurs with zero gate friction — the player never has to worry about "did I forget to scout?" after a bye.
- `bye_recovery` is present in `Aftermath` when the week was a bye: `{ summary: string, players: string[] }`. The `players` array names the players who recovered from fatigue. This field is the bye-specific story — it is not surfaced prominently today.

---

## 2. Information Hierarchy

**Primary:** The rest/recovery outcome — which players recovered and what that means for next week (`bye_recovery.summary` + `bye_recovery.players`). The bye's primary value to the player is fresher legs next week; that story should lead.

**Secondary:** Standings shift — what happened in the rest of the league while the player rested. This is the "while you were away" context.

**Cut (do not render or demote to a footnote):**
- The `ReplayTimeline` on a bye week — it has no beats and no moments. It should not render (the empty state is already correct; verify it triggers cleanly and that no collapsed-but-present shell occupies space).
- The `PrimaryFactorCard` — `primary_factor` is absent on bye weeks (confirmed from types: `primary_factor` is `undefined` on `Aftermath` when derivation not applicable). Nothing to render.
- The `MatchScoreHero` — `match_card` is null. Nothing to render.
- The "Prospect Pulse" card in `FalloutGrid` can be retained as tertiary but should be visually lower-weight than recovery and standings — prospect interest rarely moves on bye weeks and risks looking like filler.
- The "War Room · Wk N Debrief" kicker/label in `Headline` — replace with a bye-specific framing (e.g., "Bye Week · Rest Report").

## 3. Data Inventory

**Fields present on `Aftermath` for a bye week:**
- `headline`: string — server-authored bye headline (e.g., "Bye week. Your squad rests.")
- `match_card`: `null` — no match
- `bye_recovery`: `{ summary: string, players: string[] }` — present on bye weeks; names players who recovered
- `development_feedback`: may be present if training occurred during the bye
- `player_growth_deltas`: `Array<{ player_id, player_name, attribute, delta }>` — may be non-empty if bye triggered any growth
- `standings_shift`: `Array<{ club_id, club_name, old_rank, new_rank }>` — non-empty if other matches changed standings
- `recruit_reactions`: `Array<{ prospect_id, prospect_name, interest_delta, evidence }>` — may be empty on a bye
- `body`: `AftermathParagraph[]` — server-authored paragraphs
- `verdict`: optional string — may be absent on bye
- `primary_factor`: `undefined` — absent on bye weeks, do not render `PrimaryFactorCard`
- `narrative_beats`: `undefined` — absent on bye weeks
- `improvement_panel`: `undefined` — absent on bye weeks (no loss to improve from)

**Dashboard fields available from `CommandCenterSimResponse`:**
- `dashboard.week`: integer — the bye week number
- `dashboard.stage`: string — "Regular Season" (byes do not occur in playoffs)
- `dashboard.result`: typically empty or "Bye" on a bye week

**Also available from `MatchWeek` state:**
- `isBye = Boolean(aftermath.bye_recovery)` — this boolean guards bye-specific rendering

---

## 4. Constraints

- Desktop-first matrix (1440x900 / 1366x768 / 1280x720). Mobile layouts are explicitly a non-goal.
- The bye aftermath must not render a visually empty shell where the `MatchScoreHero` or `ReplayTimeline` normally lives — preserve the `null`/`undefined` guards that already skip these components.
- Semantic markup: the recovery section should be a `<section>` with a heading. The player list inside `bye_recovery.players` should be a `<ul>`.
- No new dependency, no new API call. All data is on the `Aftermath` payload that already loads from the simulate response.
- No routing change. The bye aftermath is the same route as the regular aftermath, driven by data.
- "Explain, don't decide" — surface which players recovered and why (the `summary` string is the engine's explanation). Do not author alternative recovery copy.

---

## 5. Success Criteria

1. The bye aftermath does not render any empty score hero, empty replay timeline shell, or "INCONCLUSIVE" primary factor — only content that exists for a bye week is shown.
2. `bye_recovery.players` (the specific players who rested) is surfaced with more prominence than it currently has as a `<small>` tag.
3. The `Headline` kicker communicates "rest week," not "match debrief."
4. Standings shift still renders — the player should know what happened in the rest of the league while they rested.
5. No horizontal overflow at 1280x720 minimum desktop width.
6. The advance action (proceed to next week) is reachable without scrolling past empty or irrelevant sections.
